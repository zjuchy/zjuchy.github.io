第一段

First, the author believes that the cedars have been killed by insect parasites, namely cedar bark beetles. On the contrary, the speaker points out that bettles cannot be the fundamental reason of the decline. Cedars are saturated with chemicals that are poisonous to beetles and therefore are resistant. He also explains that the dead trees found with bettles must have been damaged before the bettles eat them.

Second, the reading says the brown bears can cause the decline through eaing the barks for sugar. Nevertheless, the listening argues that the bears may influence the population but cannot be responsible for the overall decline. Yellow cedar decline on both the mainland and island, while no bears live on the latter. Therefore, bears could not be the fundamental reason of the overall decline.

Third, the writer attributed to the decline to the. climate change. Because of the changes in the temperature, the yellow cader trees now grow in late winter, which could lead to frozen roots and undermined overall health. However, the professor rejects this theory. According to him, the trees are found dying more at lower elevations where the weather is warmer and less at higher elevations where it is colder. If coldness were the reason, trees should be dying more at colder places. While in reality, this is not the case.
